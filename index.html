Aitoff Projection

The projection outline takes the form of an ellipse. Scale is correct along the equator and the central meridian. Distortion values are symmetric across the equator and the central meridian. This projection is best used for small-scale mapping not requiring accurate area measurements.

WGS84 Projection

World Geodetic System consists of a reference ellipsoid, a standard coordinate system, altitude data, and a geoid. It is used as a reference coordinate system for GPS.

Online Information Source

Pseudo-Mercator Projection

It is a conformal cylindrical map projection in which all local shapes are accurate and correctly defined at infinitesimal scale. It is best used for large-scale mapping of the areas near the equator such as Indonesia and parts of the Pacific Ocean.

Winkel I Projection

It is a compromise pseudocylindrical map projection for world maps.

Cylindrical Equal Area Projection

It shows the world as a rectangle while maintaining relative areas on a map.

Equidistant Conic Projection

It preserves distances along all meridians and two standard parallels.

Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

It preserves both distance and direction from the central point, as world is projected onto a flat surface from any point on the globe.

Bonne Projection

It is an equal-area pseudoconic map projection. Its graticule takes a form of a heart, and it was frequently used to map continents.

Cassini Projection

It is a transverse cylindrical equidistant projection. The projection maintains scale along the central meridian and all lines perpendicular to the central meridian.
